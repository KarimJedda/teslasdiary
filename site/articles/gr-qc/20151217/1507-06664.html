Distorted black holes from a vacuum five-dimensional spherical solution.

By Abraão J.S Capistrano, Antonio C. Gutiérrez-Piñeres, Sergio C. Ulhoa, Ronni G.G. Amorim

We study the deformation caused by the influence of extrinsic curvature on a vacuum spherically symmetric metric embedded in a five-dimensional bulk. In this sense, we investigate the produced stationary black-holes and derive general properties such as their masses, horizons and singularities. As a test, we also study the bending of light near such black-holes analyzing the movement of a test particle and the distortion caused by extrinsic curvature on its movement. Accordingly, using asymptotically conformal flat condition on the extrinsic curvature and an analytical expansion of a set of \emph{n}-scalar fields, we show that the corresponding black holes must be large and constrained in the range $-1/2 \leq n \leq 1.8$. As a result, they are locally thermodynamically stable, but not globally preferred.
