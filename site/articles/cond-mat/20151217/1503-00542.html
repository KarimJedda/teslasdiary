Fano fingerprints of a Kitaev chain locally off the topological phase.

By L.S. Ricco, Y. Marques, F.A. Dessotti, R.S. Machado, L.H. Guessi, M.S. Figueira, M. de Souza, A.C. Seridonio

We investigate theoretically a Fano interferometer composed by STM and AFM tips closed to superconducting adatoms, in which the adatom describing one edge of the Kitaev chain, placed under the AFM tip, encloses a pair of Majorana fermions (MFs) locally off the topological phase as aftermath of the AFM tip presence. For the binding energy $\Delta$ of the Cooper pair delocalized into the adatoms under the tips, coincident with the tunneling amplitude $t$ between them, namely $\Delta$ = $t$, we find that only one MF beneath the AFM tip hybridizes with the adatom coupled to the STM tips. As a result, a gate invariance feature different from the topological protection verified in bulk p-wave superconductors emerges: the Fano profile of the transmittance is revealed as an invariant quantity as a function of the STM tips Fermi energy, due to the symmetric swap in the gate potential of the AFM tip. Thereby in order to reveal these novel Majorana signatures, we expect that in the near future such an interferometer can be performed.
