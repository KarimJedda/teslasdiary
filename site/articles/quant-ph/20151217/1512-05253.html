Resonant interaction between two correlated atoms with frequency inside a photonic crystal bandgap.

By Valentina Notararigo, Roberto Passante, Lucia Rizzuto

We consider the resonant interaction energy and force between two identical atoms, one in an excited state and the other in the ground state, interacting with the quantum electromagnetic field in the vacuum state and placed inside a photonic crystal. The two atoms are prepared in their symmetric or antisymmetric entangled state; the atomic excitation is thus distributed among the atoms. This problem is also strictly related to the coherent resonant energy transfer between two atoms. We consider the case of an atomic transition frequency inside the frequency gap of the photonic crystal and in proximity of the lower edge of its first band gap. We show that in this case the resonant interaction energy asymptotically decreases as $1/{r^2}$, compared with the $1/r$ dependence in both cases of free space and atomic frequency outside the photonic crystal gap. We argue that, although the resonant force is reduced by the presence of the photonic crystal, the configuration considered can be a good candidate for the observation of the resonant interaction because spontaneous emission is strongly suppressed and the correlated state is thus preserved by the decoherence effect due to its spontaneous decay.
