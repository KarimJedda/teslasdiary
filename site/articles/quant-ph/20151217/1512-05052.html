Noise effects on enhancing realistic controlled remote state preparation.

By Yuan-hua Li, Hua-qing Zhou, Zisheng Wang, Xian-feng Chen

We investigate how the efficiency is affected on the controlled remote state preparation protocol in noise environment, where all possible types of noise are considered in realistic quantum communication protocols. We discuss the several realistic scenarios, i.e., a part and all of the qubits are subjected to the same or different types of noise. We find that more noise or less entanglement of qubits environment lead to more efficiency in terms of average fidelity. Furthermore, we show that a better approach to subject the qubits in different noise channels in order to obtain an increase of the efficiency for the protocol.
