Unifying Frameworks for Nonlocality and Contextuality.

By Nadish de Silva

There are two frameworks, due to Cabello-Severini-Winter and Abramsky-Brandenburger-Hardy, for studying Bell and noncontextual inequalities; they differ vastly in their mathematical approaches. We unify their approaches and give simple, graph-theoretic methods for finding inequality-free proofs of nonlocality or contextuality and for finding states exhibiting strong nonlocality or contextuality. Finally, we apply these methods to concrete examples in stabilizer quantum mechanics relevant to understanding contextuality as a resource in quantum computation via magic state distillation.
