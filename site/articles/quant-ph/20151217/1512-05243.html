Relaxation after quenches in systems with photon-mediated long-range interactions.

By Stefan Schütz, Simon B. Jäger, Giovanna Morigi

We study relaxation after quenches across a driven-dissipative phase transition, which is characterized by conservative and dissipative long-range forces. The long-range forces are mediated by the photons of a single-mode cavity and can organize the atoms in regular patterns. The quench is realised by suddenly tuning the strength and frequency of the control field, here a laser that pumps atoms, across the critical value of the selforganization transition. The dynamics following this sudden quench exhibits long-lived metastable states with non-Gaussian momentum distributions. We characterize their onset and evolution towards equilibrium and show that, while the metastable non-thermal distribution is prevailingly due to the coherent long-range interactions, thermalization can be further slowed down by the incoherent dynamics. In particular, the incoherent processes leading to equilibrium are characterized by relaxation times that are orders of magnitude larger than the ones solely due to elastic photon-mediated collisions. This behaviour, that challenges the common understanding of the onset of equilibrium, is a peculiar property of the long-range interactions in this setup.
